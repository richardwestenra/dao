Written back in 2000,

2000

Before my time, so if I’ve got anything wrong, let me know.

The year 2000 was the dark ages of web development

The First Browser War was just winding down, IE 5 was crushing Netscape 4.5,
and people were still arguing about whether this newfangled CSS thing would take off.

At the time, there was a culture war

Print Designers

versus

Web Designers

Most web designers were originally print designers.

They were used to complete control

The web is a flexible medium

Web page might look completely different from one computer to the next.

  • Screen sizes & resolutions
  • Colours, brightness & contrast
  • System fonts
  • Custom default font-sizes
  • Browser rendering
  • Competing web standards

Print designers hated this chaos, they wanted pixel perfect control.

The Chaos is a strength

What John Allsop argued is the chaos is a strength

The web's flexibility & ubiquity aren’t problems, they're possibilities.

Make the web accessible, and allow uses we could not have imagined 15 years ago.

So now it’s 2015. We live in the future!
Self-driving cars!
Occulus Rifts!
Whatever this is!

New tools

  • Media queries
  • Webfonts
  • Browser prefixes (and Autoprefixer!)
  • Modernizr
  • CanIUse.com

On the one hand, we now have all these fancy tools, APIs and frameworks, and it’s now faster and easier than ever to make really terrible websites.

New problems

On the other hand, we now have dozens of browsers and hundreds of versions running on thousands of devices.

We’ve all got smartphones in our pockets, and tablets, and phablets, and smart watches,

New tools?

and you can even browse the web on your glasses if you don't mind looking like a tool.

Perfection
is hard

We now have great power, but we still don't have control

The Law of Diminishing Returns

Economic principle: Loosely states that as you invest more towards a goal, you get less return on each unit invested.

80/20 rule: I tend to find that takes me about 20% of my time to complete 80% of a project, and completing the last fifth takes 80% of the effort.

"the gain is not worth the pain."

Cost-benefit calculations

  • How necessary is the work?
  • How many users? (CanIUse / Analytics)
  • How crucial? Will anyone die? Government, medical, Apple etc.
  • What is the opportunity cost?

Know when to let it go
